Meet Steven

Writing a senior thesis provides a wonderful opportunity for Political Science majors to do independent research in social science. It also provides a "capstone" experience for the major. The program involves independent work by the student, guided by a faculty advisor, and enrollment in a senior thesis seminar during the fall and spring semesters of a student's senior year. A successful thesis will be roughly the size of a journal article: between 25 and 50 pages. The size of the thesis is only a guide; actual lengths will vary widely by subfield and advisor. Although all Political Science majors may write a thesis regardless of their GPA, majors who wish to graduate with Latin Honors (cum laude, magna cum laude, and summa cum laude) must write a senior thesis and maintain a cumulative GPA of 3.65 and above to qualify. Latin Honors will then be based on the final grade awarded to the thesis, with a maximum level determined by the student's overall GPA relative to other Latin Honors candidates, as stipulated by the College of Arts & Sciences.
